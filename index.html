What is Co-Marketing

Co-marketing can be beneficial for any brand that wants to broaden their audience, increase brand awareness, and offer a new type of content. It's the process of growing two or more businesses at the same time by working together to share expertise and offer value to their audiences. Partner companies typically collaborate on promotional efforts for a co-branded offer. In a co-marketing partnership, both companies promote a piece of content or product, and share the results of that promotion. By leveraging the relationship and reach of a partner, co-marketing campaigns are designed to deliver more awareness, leads, and more sales conversions.

Co-branding Campaign: Project Rock

One of the biggest benefits of co-branding campaigns is the chance to expose your product or service to a brand new audience. That's exactly what Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson and Under Armour did when they partnered together back in 2016. Together, the two brands created an exclusive product line of gym bags, shoes, and workout apparel. While Under Armour has been its own brand for fitness enthusiasts, so has the Rock. He has been branded since his days spent in the WWE and it has followed on into his acting and business ventures. With an iconic figure like Dwayne Johnson displaying such physical prowess and being a role model for his work ethic in the gym, this partnership was set in motion with the concrete belief that the collection would bring immense awareness and generate sales.

The reason brands tend to team up in co-branding is that their audience will already have some sort of overlap. Even if it is small. And in this case there was such a direct overlap with millions of people following Dwayne Johnson across his social platforms for the motivation to either begin or continue their fitness journeys, that this was a match made in heaven for collaboration. This extremely useful component of marketing strategy can be applied to any industry as long as the entities involved bring something to table and can both benefit.
